How I work

Every voice in the room, early.

I facilitate before I design — getting each voice heard and integrated until the organization starts behaving like an organism that can sense collectively. That's when work comes alive and aligns without loose ends. It's also taught me to stay open: the best product insights arrive from unexpected places.

Rough edges first.

I test early, iterate, test again. I love lo-fi prototyping — rough edges give people room to imagine into the possibilities and be earnest about what they actually want. Polish too early and everyone politely agrees with it.

Evidence at the edge.

Most of my work lives at the edge of what's possible, so I give it roots: mixed-methods user research plus the academic desk work that connects product decisions to the literature. I think in systems and take the meta view — mapping happy paths, surfacing choke points, and defining where AI belongs. Designed well, AI is a symbiont that strengthens collective intelligence; I build with my own tools, under my own direction.

Hiring a design lead who ships?

I'm open to founding designer, design lead, and head of design roles — especially 0→1 and AI-native products. Remote, or Los Angeles.
